# HR Analytics Dashboard

## About the Project

This project was created to explore how data analytics can be used to understand workforce trends and support better HR decision-making.

Using Power BI, I analyzed employee-related data to identify patterns in attrition, job satisfaction, performance, promotions, and workforce demographics. The goal was to transform raw HR data into meaningful insights through interactive dashboards and visualizations.

Through this project, I gained hands-on experience in data cleaning, KPI tracking, dashboard design, and business-focused reporting.

---

## Problem Statement

Employee retention and workforce management are important challenges for every organization. HR teams need clear insights into employee behavior, satisfaction levels, and performance trends to make informed decisions.

This project focuses on answering questions such as:

* Which departments experience the highest attrition?
* How does employee satisfaction vary across the organization?
* Are promotions and performance related?
* What workforce trends can help improve retention?

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains information related to:

* Employee demographics
* Department and job roles
* Performance ratings
* Satisfaction levels
* Promotion history
* Salary information
* Employee attrition status

Before creating the dashboard, the data was cleaned and organized to ensure accurate analysis and reporting.

## Dashboard Analysis

### Employee Attrition

Analyzed employee turnover across different departments and job roles to identify areas with higher attrition rates.

### Employee Satisfaction

Explored satisfaction levels across the workforce and compared trends between departments.

### Performance & Promotions

Examined employee performance ratings and promotion history to understand workforce growth patterns.

### Workforce Demographics

Studied workforce distribution based on age, gender, and department to gain demographic insights.

---

## Key Insights

Some of the insights generated from the analysis include:

* Certain departments showed higher employee attrition than others.
* Employee satisfaction varied significantly across teams and roles.
* Promotion trends appeared to influence retention in several cases.
* Workforce demographics provided additional context for understanding employee behavior.
